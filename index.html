You are considered fully trained (50% of a skill level) if the trainer says you must practice more when you attempt to train.

Magic skill increases per point spent, weapon skill increases per swing.

Black Broadsword

Combat Adds +5, AC +4, blue glow
The Black Broadsword is usually the first sought weapon for most classes.
To get a Black Broadsword, you must first obtain the rocks to make it.
You'll need 1 iron ore rock and 1 ytrill rock to make 1 sword.
Iron ore can be found in -4 Kesmai but more commonly in Axe Glacier near the town.

Ytrill ore can be found in -4 Kesmai, the lair of Trog in Kesmai, and near Axe Glacier town.

Once you obtain both of these rocks, take them to Ironbar.
Ironbar is located to the east of the recall ring seller of Kesmai, across a bridge and just past the pit that goes to Kesmai -3.
Around 30 hexes east of the recall ring seller in total, you'll see his building to the south.
Put the 2 rocks at his feet and say "Ironbar, sell broadsword". He will then drop a Black Broadsword for you to take.

Infused Black Broadsword (IBBS)

Combat Adds +5, AC +5, blue glow
Bring a heavy silver weapon for this quest!
To upgrade your black broadsword to a demonically infused black broadsword, you must travel to Oakvael -6 and slay the gold dragon Wendy. You must land the killing blow. This will impress Sorceress Althacrex at the end of the gauntlet, who will upgrade your sword.
Get killing blow on Wendy, drop BBS at Althacrex's feet, and type "S, teach".

Crystalline Gauntlets

CG1

CG1: Combat adds +5, AC +4, blue glow

To obtain the Crystalline Gauntlets, visit Konton in the SW corner of Oakvael surface and type "Konton, teach". She will tell you to find her brother, Konran, in Oakvael Troll Town.
Konran is slightly north of the stairs to Oakvael UD in troll town. Tell him "Konran, teach" and he will tell you of Drizulu and Glamuzu, the deputy and sheriff of Troll Town.
You must get the killing blow on Glamuzu, but anyone can kill Drizulu. Pick up the key that one of them will drop, bring it back to Konran.
Drop the key on the ground, type "Konran, teach" and pick up the cloth he gives you. Bring the cloth to Torii.
Drop cloth for Torii, type "Torii, teach".
You must now go to Leng and land the killing blow on Shidosha. Get the buddha that drops and bring it to Puck in Leng.
Drop the buddha and type "Puck, teach" and you will get your crystalline gauntlets!

CG2

CG2: Combat adds +5, AC +5, blue glow
Yuri, now meditating in the very deepest pit in Oakvael, will teach MAs how to block better with their CGs (and deal a touch more damage), if they are sturdy enough to make it to her.
You must make it past Wendy located on Oakvael -6 to reach Yuri for the upgrade. Type "Yuri, teach" to receive your CG2 upgrade.
Bring a party to make it past Wendy in Oakvael -6.

CG3

CG3: Combat adds +6, AC +6, blue glow
You must land the killing blow on the Golem. Golem cannot be hurt by magic or edged weapons. Jump down north from the middle of the bridge in Leng (where you can't see land on either side), head north and you'll see the rope to golem. Bring a party.
After golem is dead, do NOT facet. Climb back down the rope, go west once, drop your CG2 gauntlets and say "S, teach". You will be rewarded with the CG3s.

Hummingbird Longsword Quest

Combat adds +6, AC +2, swings 1.5x faster than normal
Hummingbird Amulet from TrollKing to get the sword.
The Hummingbird Longsword is usually the last sought weapon because of the difficulty to get one. To get the sword, you must first obtain the amulet. The amulet is dropped by the Troll King in UnderKingdom -300.
After slaying the Troll King go through a secret door in the southeast corner and go north. Step on the black hex to be teleported to a room with an NPC in the middle of a ring of fire. In addition to the NPC, there are a couple sets of hard hitting skeletons with high HP called "Remains". Take the amulet to the NPC, place it on the ground, and type "npc, train". He will drop the longsword.

Returning Axe Quest

Combat adds +5, AC +2, returns when thrown, blue glow, pierces armor.
Obsidian Egg from the King Wolf (KWolf). Give to Vulcan for RAxe.
The Returning Axe is sought after you obtain the Returning Hammer. Even though the stats are the same, it can penetrate armor more often. To get the Returning Axe, you must first obtain the obsidian egg. The egg is only dropped by the Kwolf in Axe Glacier. The Kwolf is usually found wandering alone and can only be hurt by silver weapons. Silver Dagger, Returning Dagger, Silver Greataxe, etc. The Kwolf roams alone (not in a pack of wolves), but there are Pwolves (Prince wolves) as well. You'll know you've found the Kwolf by how hard it hits.
Once you obtain the Obsidian Egg, take it to Vulcan. Vulcan is located through a portal in Axe Glacier southeast of the eastern Drake rope. In that area, it is the farthest south hex surrounded by 3 stone walls. This is the portal. You will need to hold a moonstine ring in your right hand and it must be "night time" to enter the portal. Vulcan's daughter will try to steal the egg, so just hit her or drop some coins on the floor to keep her busy. Put the egg at his feet and say "Vulcan, sell axe" and he will give you the returning axe.

Returning Hammer

Combat Adds +5, AC +2
The Returning Hammer is usually sought by classes that need their first ranged attack.
To get one, you have three choices:
  1. Behind Daisy's Lair. After slaying her, go southwest through the green water. There will be 12+ baby dragons and pirahnas to kill. Go through the door to the north, it will be on the eastern most hex. It is not always there.
  2. Found on the table in Ydnac's lair. Not always there. Bring death protection for Ydnac, and watch out for his pet tiger.
  3. Slay the Giant in Axe Glacier, he can drop the Returning Hammer. Most difficult method of the three.

Silver Greataxe

Combat Adds +5, AC +1, poor blocking, heavy silver
The Silver Greataxe is usually sought out because certain monsters/lair monsters can only be hurt with silver weapons. With stats of +5 AC+1 makes it a hard hitter, but doesn't block that well. To get a Silver Greataxe, you must first obtain the rocks to make it. You'll need 1 iron ore rock and 1 ytrill rock to make 1 axe. These rocks are usually found scattered throughout Kesmai -4 and more commonly found in Axe Glacier.
Once you obtain both of these rocks, take them to Silvermoon. Silvermoon is located through a portal in Axe Glacier town. From the portal landing area go south and there will be a single hex on the west wall. This is the portal, you will need to hold a Moonstone ring in your right hand and it has to be "Night Time" to enter the portal. Put the 2 rocks at his feet and say "Silvermoon, sell greataxe". He will then drop a Silver Greataxe for you to take.

Becoming a Knight

In order to accept the quest for Knighthood you must be at least a level 8 Fighter and seek out either Knight trainer. For starters its unlikely that a level 8 Fighter can survive the trip to either trainer alone. It would be in your best interest to get help.

There are 2 ways to gain Knighthood:
  1. Hermann who is located in a cavern in the far northeastern region of the Kesmai surface. You will encounter Orc's, Boars, Wolves, Crocodiles, Bears, Centaurs, Griffin's
  2. Aniet who is in a pit located deep within the thick forest in the northeastern region of the Oakvael surface. You will encounter Crocodiles, KillerBees, Beetles, Orc's, Bears, Boars, Wolves.
Once there, stand on their hex, with your right hand empty, and ask them to train you. For Hermann type "Hermann, train" and for Aniet type "Aniet, train"

Congratulations!! You are now a Knight. Your icon changed and a Knight ring will appear. Take the ring and wear it. The Knight ring when worn will give you the only spells Knights will ever get, which are Cure, Locate, Strength, Light, and Enchant Weapon.

While you are there you might as well buy a couple extra rings for 150 coins each, drop the coins the trainer's hex and type "Hermann, sell ring" or "Aniet, sell ring"

Knight's 1st Quest (37 Max HP)

This gem comes from Daisy in Kesmai -4 and is required for the quest.
If soloing, wait until at least level 12 and bring fire protection.
  1. Train with Cudyl (Oakvael)
  2. Kill Daisy, take the Agate Gem (Kesmai)
  3. Bring Agate Gem to Harriette and Train (Kesmai)
  4. You Gain 37 Max Hit Points

To start this Quest you must see Cudyl in Oakvael. Cudyl is located to the far east of the portal landing area. The easiest way to find him is to use your locate spell. Once there stand on his hex and type "Cudyl, train". He will ask you if you want the quest then type "Cudyl, yes". He will tell you to go slay the small dragon. The small dragon is Daisy located in Kesmai-4. Slay Daisy and take the Agate Gem (the gem ties to the person who picks it up) to Harriette in Kesmai. Harriette is next to Hermann who is located in a cavern in the far northeastern region of the Kesmai surface. Put the gem at her feet and type "Harriette, train", she will give you an extra 37 max hit points.

Knight's 2nd Quest (10 Max HP)

The Axe Glacier Yeti's dagger is required for this quest.
Silver is required for killing Wendy of Oakvael -6. Bring the SGA!
  1. Teach with Brych (Oakvael)
  2. Kill Yeti, take Dagger (Axe Glacier)
  3. Bring Dagger to Brych and Teach (Oakvael)
  4. Take Halberd to Gorffwyltera and Teach (Oakvael)
  5. You Gain 10 Max Hit Points

To start this Quest you must see Brych in Oakvael. Stand on his hex and type "Brych, teach" he will tell you to "bring me the special dagger". Now off to Axe Glacier to kill the Yeti for the Dagger. On your way to the Yeti you will encounter the Axe Drake and Mama the Ice Dragon. You can gather a high level party to help slay the Drake, Mama, and Yeti. Or have a high level thief slay the Drake for you and help you run past Mama, and then have the thief slay the Yeti. Like the Agate gem the Dagger ties to the person who picks it up. Take the Dagger back to Brych and put it at his feet and type "Brych, teach". He will take the Dagger and give you the Knight Halberd. Now off to see Gorf in the lower levels of Undead in Oakvael. On your way you will have to run through a fire room that has a Wyrm in it. After that there is a secret door that contains 4 Minotaurs that can only be hurt by the Silver Greataxe. Then there's another Wyrm before you get to Wendy. Now the greatest challenge is defeating Wendy, like the Minotaurs she can only be harmed with the Silver Greataxe. Her magic abilities are disabled at this writing, but she hits just as hard as the Ice Dragon. After defeating Wendy there will be 3 concussion casting spectres on your way to Gorf. Put the halberd at Gorffwyltera's feet and type "Gorffwyltera, teach" he will take the halberd and give you an extra 10 max hit points.

Underworld (Praetoseba) Quests

Entering UW

Underworld (Praetoseba) is usually done to restore skill levels and constitution lost from death, and also to restore your youth. It is also required if you are eaten by evil carniverous creatures such as dragons.
You have to enter underworld from a black hex located in the graveyard of each land (in Axe Glacier the hex is a green gravemarker by the statue southwest of the portals), stand on the hex and type the chant in command mode: urruku ya zi xul
Entering UW drops ALL OF YOUR ITEMS on the hex you entered it on. LOCKER YOUR GEAR FIRST!
All commands are "npc name", teach (ex. "osiris, teach" or "o, teach" without quotes)
To accept quest "npc name, yes" (ex. "osiris, yes" or "o, yes" without quotes)

You start in UW in tall yellow grass, go south along the path, through the door, to the sky hexes, jump. Now you're in the main temple.

Stomach Quest

Find Osiris - West of starting hex in the main temple.
Type "Osiris, teach" He will ask, "Do you wish to quest for a stomach?" Type "Osiris, yes"
Response 1: Give a suitable pet figurine to my son.
Go east on the north river bank to sky hexes, then southeast across bridge, then north to the forest, avoid the archer unless you need the bow for the Intestines Quest. Kill the tiger and loot the figurine. Might as well kill the bear for the skull so you will have night vision or if you happen to need it for the Intestines Quest. Kill self and you will be back in the main temple, go southwest from the fountain and give the tiger figurine to Horus, he will give you an amulet to give to Isis. Go north to Isis and give her the amulet, then go east to Imhotep and he will give you a stomach.
Response 2: Get an ankh from one who was queen.
Go east on the north river bank past the first building, go north, to the second door on the east and speak with Tiy, and she will give you the ankh. Take the ankh to Nepherteri who is directly north across a little river, she will have you talk to Imhotep back in the main temple to get your stomach.
Response 3: Go inquire of the locksmith.
Go east on the south river bank to the first building, talk to Khenty he will give you a key. Take the key to Thutmose the Magician (northwest from the temple, swim up the little river, then east along the south bank to the door, then south) give him the key and he will tell you to talk to Imhotep back in the main temple to get your stomach.

Intestines Quest

Find Anubis - Go east along the north river bank, inside the first building.
Type "Anubis, teach" He will ask, "Do you wish to quest for a intestines?" Type "Anubis, yes"
Response 1: Take a special bow to the Goddess of the Hunt.
Go east on the north river bank to sky hexes, then southeast across bridge, then east, kill archer and take bow to Neith (northwest back across bridge, then west look for tall yellow grass) and she will give you intestines.
Response 2: Give the skull of a bear to the God of the River.
If you had the tiger quest you should already have the skull, if not then Go east on the north river bank to sky hexes, then southeast across bridge, then north to the forest, watch out for the archer, kill bear and take skull to Sobek (small island south of the fourth building from the temple from the north river bank) then see either the West Nymph (southwest from Sobek surrounding by trees on the river bank) or East Nymph (southeast from Sobek surrounded by trees). Take the gem from the Nymph to Nehebku back in the main temple, southeast of the fountain and he will give you intestines.

Liver Quest

Find Sekhmet - Go east along the south river bank inside the third building, down stairs - see Ra or Nefertem
Type "Sekhmet, teach" He will ask, "Do you wish to quest for a liver?" Type "Sekhmet, yes"
Response 1: The Sun God shall be your guide.
Jump in Fire hex in Sekhmet's room. You will be back in the main temple. Go NW or SW from fountain to stairs and climb up. This is Ra's chambers, go talk with Ra and he will tell you to "Seek the wisdom of Ptah." OR "Lead a lawful cobra to Re.Harakhti."
"Lead a lawful cobra to Re.Harakhti."
Kill cobra in Ra's chamber, take boots, climb down rope NE of Sekhmet's building, go northwest, then north, find Re.Harakhti, give him the boots.
"Seek the wisdom of Ptah."
Go east along the south river bank to sky hexes, then swim north to the island, climb down rope, then east southeast at the end of the cavern, see Ptah, kill self, then go down the stairs, then on the far west wall of the temple is a door, Thoth is in there.
Response 2: Inquire of my brother.
Nefertem is Sekhmet's brother. Seek him out. Otherwise, the quest is identical to Ra. You will either lead the cobra, or find Ptah. See Response 1 above.

Kesmai Lairs

Librarian
  1. Weakness: Any
  2. Hit Points: Low to Moderate
  3. Damage: 10-24
  4. Attacks: Weapon swing, knockback
  5. Magic Abilities: None
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 1
  7. Loot: Diamonds to sell

Head down to -1 Kesmai and travel left until you reach the left side of the the map. Head north and there will be a rat, this is how you will know you've arrived. Inside and to the east, there's a small room which usually houses 1 random mob and the Librarian. He can be trouble to newer players due to his knockback which can stun you into walls, but other than that he's just a beefier skeleton.
Trog
  1. Weakness: Any
  2. Hit Points: Very few
  3. Damage: 1-20
  4. Attacks: Knockback, Mace, Kick
  5. Magic Abilities: None
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 3
  7. Loot:

Trog is the King of the Trolls in Kesmai, and resides in his lair above Kesmai -2. He is probably the easiest lair monster to kill, and the one most people kill first. He fights like a troll and does not use any spells, although he has more hit points and takes longer to kill-plus he can knock you off his lair so move away from the air hex.
Daisy
  1. Weakness: Any
  2. Hit Points: Moderate
  3. Damage: 20-50
  4. Attacks: Claws, tail, Wings
  5. Magic Abilities: Fireball
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 6
  7. Loot:

Daisy is one of the most seeked out lair creatures, for the fact that she is an integral part of the Knight's Quest and sometimes holds a returning hammer in the back of her lair. Make sure that you have plenty of fire protect before attempting to fight Daisy, as she casts powerful fire spells often through the fight. If you are a Magic User who helps a knight to fight Daisy, bring plenty of balms because Daisy centers her attacks on the Magic Users in the group. A group of 4 who cooperate well should be able to beat Daisy, but be careful because her lair is a no-recall zone. which means, if you are in trouble you have to run all the way out of the lair just to escape. After killing Daisy, be sure to take the corpse to the tanner to get a nice set of dragon scale armor.
Ydnac
  1. Weakness: Blue Glow
  2. Hit Points: Moderate
  3. Damage: 40-90 with death, 10-20 with weapon
  4. Attacks: Staff
  5. Magic Abilities: Death, Stun
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 6
  7. Loot:

Ydnac is located southeast of Hermann's (which is where knights go to complete the Agate Quest). He resides in his lair with his tiger pet. Ydnac has very powerful death spells, and if you have no death protection, he can easily take 90-100 with one death spell. The easiest way to beat him is to wait around the corner of his lair, warm an attack spell (death works well) move around the corner and hit him with it to fizzle his spell, then run back around the corner before he can hit you. Repeat this strategy, but keep in mind he may try to chase you so get far away. The returning hammer is located on the altar but is not a guaranteed drop.
Great.White
  1. Weakness: Immune to Death
  2. Hit Points: Moderate
  3. Damage: 30-50
  4. Attacks: Charges (Jumpkick), Bites
  5. Magic Abilities: None
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 6
  7. Loot: Tanned for Heavy Jacket f/i +3, Waterbreathing, Mana Regen +1

Head east of outside of Kesmai, past Ironbar's house, and there will be a spooked.rat next to the water. Swim southeast and you'll see the Great.White. He can move and attack in the same round so be careful. Death spells do not work on him, further testing needed.

Oakvael Lairs

Doom.Orc
  1. Weakness: Anything
  2. Hit Points: Moderate
  3. Damage: 10-30
  4. Attacks: Doom Crossbow
  5. Magic Abilities: None
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 5
  7. Loot:

Doom.Orc is the first lair crit thieves seek out, because he holds the coveted +4 glowing crossbow that all thieves need. Magic Users(Thaums and Wizards) are not allowed into his lair at all, so only Knights, MAs, and Thieves can get in. He has about 3 or 4 orc companions that carry crossbows as well, but they are not +4 like the one he carries. A presence can also usually be found around him, which can make it very difficult for thieves to stay hidden in his lair. To enter his lair(which is SE of the portals) It must be night time.
Serpent
  1. Weakness: Blue glow, most spells
  2. Hit Points: Moderate to high moderate
  3. Damage: 30-75
  4. Attacks: Bite, above average stun rate
  5. Magic Abilities: Poison
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 7
  7. Loot: Used to drop DPs, now drops nothing but gold.

The Oakvael serpent is a monster that is an exception to the rule. It does not actually have a "lair," but rather wanders around the Serpent Level (-3) of Oakvael. It can be rather difficult to defeat, depending on what skills you have. It can be hurt by glowing weapons. Ice Spear and Death work as well. The serpent also has a very strong poison. It can stun easily, so preferably fight it alone. The serpent will run when it's close to death, so it's advisable to bring it to the hidden room west of the Troll Town stairs before killing it. The serpent does not drop any loot besides gold.
Lich Twins
  1. Weakness: Anything except Magic (Ice spear?)
  2. Hit Points: Moderate
  3. Damage: 20-50
  4. Attacks: Fists
  5. Magic Abilities: Fireball
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 7
  7. Loot:

The Iron Liches are a pair of Liches that live on the Undead level of Oakvael. It is said that casting spells while on the Undead level attracts them toward you, especially if they have been let out of their lair by another adventurer. Their lair is located in the southern area of Undead, and is hidden by a secret door. When opened, both liches will immediately see you and start to attack you. They attack with their hands, and sometimes cast fire. One of the liches, the one with a yellow robe on, is much weaker than the other and should be killed first. After that, concentrate on the more powerful lich using your glowing weapons. Death and other attack spells do not work on them. They will hit you against the wall and knock you down, so be sure you bring lots of flasks in case you get low on hit points quickly. Once they are defeated, another secret door within their lair leads to the treasure room. In it, you can find a permanent dexterity potion, Feather Fall boots, a restore mana potion and other little items.
Wendy / Windy
  1. Weakness: Heavy Silver (SGA, golem weapons, etc)
  2. Hit Points: High
  3. Damage: N/A
  4. Attacks: Dragon Attacks (DOESN'T EAT)
  5. Magic Abilities: Whirlwind (currently disabled?)
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 9
  7. Loot: Tanned into the same scales as daisy

Wendy the Whirlwind Dragon is an integral part of the second Knight's Quest, the MA's CG2 quest, and other classes as well. It is located on the bottom floor of Oakvael, known as the Reptile Pit. You must get through the challenge gauntlet to reach her. The Whirlwind dragon usually only casts whirlwind when the fight begins, and then attacks after that. The main danger is not from the dragon itself, but rather the 4 super powerful wyrms that wander around near her. She has no loot, except that she can be tanned into dragon scales.

Leng Lairs

Sandy
  1. Weakness: Blue glow, immune to death/stun(?)
  2. Hit Points: Low to moderate
  3. Damage: 25-40
  4. Attacks: Occasional knockback
  5. Magic Abilities: None
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 4
  7. Loot:

Portal into Leng and head northwest down the stairs through undead hallway. Beware of wraiths and spectres as they blind and death. Head all the way east until you can't anymore, then head north to the top wall and inspect the wall to your right. Sandy hits for an average of 25-40 and can knockback. Make sure to bring blue glowing weapons. Might be immune to death and stun spells. The loot includes troll hide armor (+1 plate), some +1 shielders, some gems and gold. Sometimes drops the red ball which casts fireball upon throwing it.

Axe Glacier Lairs

King Wolf
  1. Weakness: Silver, immune to magic(?)
  2. Hit Points: Moderate
  3. Damage: 15-30 with physical
  4. Attacks: Teeth
  5. Magic Abilities: None
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 5
  7. Loot:

The King Wolf is another lair monster like the Serpent, where it does have an actual "lair" but rather wanders around Axe-Glacier. Because it is never in one place, adventurers often have hard time finding it. The reason many people try to kill it is because it holds an obsidian egg, the component needed to make an Raxe. The King Wolf can only be damaged by silver weapons, such as a Silver Greataxe or a silver dagger. It looks like any other wolf, so the only way to tell it is the King Wolf is to see how hard it hits and how hard it is to kill. Unless you are an experienced user of an SGA or a dagger, the King Wolf can be difficult to defeat. There are also Prince wolves (pwolf) which roam solo instead of packs, and they do not drop the eggs.

UnderKingdom Lairs

Drake
  1. Weakness: Silver, immune to magic(?)
  2. Hit Points: Average
  3. Damage: 30-80
  4. Attacks: Drake stuff (Eats?)
  5. Magic Abilities: Lightning Bolt
  6. Difficulty Rating (1-10): 8
  7. Loot:

Roams around the town surface level of UnderKingdom. If you bring him to town watch out, as he can lightning bolt through the protection of the town gate. Has more HP than Axe Glacier drake but less than Leng. Try to separate him from the wyverns and fight near town so you can escape in a pinch.
